Fourth Year Design Project – LiMar

Our fourth year design project, LiMar, was to create modular add-on for sports field lining carts which would allow the operator maintain the sport field lines/markings in an easy, consistent and yet affordable manner.

The design made use of 3D printed fixtures designed in SolidWorks, a graphical user interface designed in Python, and a system that made use of Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) to provide assistance to the operator.

Tech Art Project – Puzzle of Secrets

An art installation made for an art exhibition that focused specifically on Tech Art. The Puzzle of Secrets aims to highlight how everyone is like a puzzle that needs effort to be solved before learning of their secrets. This could be something as simple as their favourite food or something more complex such as their fears and anxieties.

The laser cut puzzle is comprised of multiple password locked boxes, which takes advantage of a custom 3D printed locking mechanism to keep the secrets safe. The electronics consisted of an Arduino, LEDs, servo motors, and switches.

3D Printable Spider Quad Robot Concept

A CAD model concept design of a 3D printable spider quad-robot made using a top-down design approach. It took advantage of a special four-bar linkage called the "Chebyshev lambda linkage" to control the legs.

This concept design and the motion studies were all completed in SolidWorks.

Vision System for Clumped Box Case

Designed and tested a way to improve a pre-existing conveyor system. The original system would be able to identify and sort boxes on the conveyor belts, based on the shapes seen on the boxes. However, this system was limited by the fact that the case of clumped boxes (boxes too close together) would cause inaccuracies. The new system, aimed to detect and divert boxes that were clumped, so as to prevent inaccurate sorting.

This system was designed and programmed in python using the OpenCV for the image detection, MQTT for the communication between the Jetson Nano and the conveyor PLCs, and Ignition 8 designer for the HMI design.

Hydraulic Lowrider DIY Kit

Designed and prototyped a model kit of a 1964 Chevy Impala lowrider using laser cut cardboard and plywood. This kit was used at The STEAM Project summer camp as one of their projects.

This kit was designed in illustrator, while testing and rapid prototyping was completed using the Epilog Mini laser cutter.

Aquadrone – Barrel Roll System Concept

A CAD model concept design of a unique system to facilitate a barrel roll designed for the Aquadrone Design Team. This system takes advantage of an asymmetrical weight distribution, which can be rotated to allow for the cylindrical mini sub to complete a barrel roll.

This concept design was created in SolidWorks using parts sourced from McMaster Carr. Mathematical calculations were completed to determine the mass and type of motor required for the system to work.
