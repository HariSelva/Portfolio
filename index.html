Featured Projects 💻

Fourth Year Design Project – LiMar

Our fourth year design project, LiMar, was to create modular add-on for sports field lining carts which would allow the operator maintain the sport field lines/markings in an easy, consisten and yet affordable manner.

The design made use of 3D printed fixtures designed in SolidWorks, a graphical user interface designed in Python, and a system that made use of Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) to provide assistance to the operator.

Read more about LiMar here.

Tech Art Project – Puzzle of Secrets

An art installation made for an art exhibition that focused specifically on Tech Art. The Puzzle of Secrets aims to showcase how everyone is like a puzzle that needs effort to be solved before learning of their secrets. This could be something as simple as their favourite food or something more complex such as their fears and anxieties.

The laser cut puzzle is comprised of multiple password locked boxes, which takes advantage of a custom 3D printed locking mechanism to keep the secrets safe. The electronics consisted of an Arduino, LEDs, servo motors, and switches.

View on GitHub. Read more about the Puzzle of Secrets here.

Hydraulic Lowrider DIY Kit

Designed and prototyped a model kit of a 1964 Chevy Impala lowrider using laser cut cardboard and plywood. This kit was used at The STEAM Project summer camp as one of their project.

This kit was designed in illustrator, while testing and rapid prototyping was completed using the Epilog Mini laser cutter.

3D Printed Fixtures
